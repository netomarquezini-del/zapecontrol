## 1. AD CREATIVE VISUAL TRENDS 2025-2026

### What's Converting Best on Meta

**Static images still drive 60-70% of conversions on Meta.** Despite video dominance in engagement, static remains critical for:
- Bottom-of-funnel / retargeting (clear offer + CTA outperforms video)
- Awareness refreshes
- Quick decision moments (user doesn't need to watch; value communicated instantly)

**Top-Performing Visual Styles:**

1. **Organic/Native-Looking Content** - Ads that don't look like ads outperform polished creatives by up to 40%. Selfie-style, screenshot-style, and casual formats win.

2. **Text-Only Static** - Images featuring only text (no photos) are trending and converting well. Think tweet screenshots, note-style posts, quote cards.

3. **UGC-Inspired Statics** - Photos that look user-generated (iPhone quality, natural lighting, real environments) vs studio shots.

4. **Meme-Style Formats** - Using familiar meme templates with product messaging inserted organically.

5. **Screenshot/Social Proof Style** - Screenshots of reviews, DMs, results dashboards, testimonials.

**Key Principle: True variation wins.** Radically different angles, tones, and creative archetypes perform better than minor iterations of the same concept.

### Design Specs
- **Aspect Ratios:** Always 1:1 or 4:5 (mobile-first)
- **Refresh Cycle:** Every 2-3 weeks (Meta learning phase resets with creative fatigue)
- **Testing Split:** 70% proven formats / 20% new experiments / 10% cutting-edge

## 2. LOW-TICKET DIGITAL PRODUCT ADS ($10-50)

### What Works for Courses, Ebooks, Digital Products

**Creative Formats That Convert:**
- **Carousel Ads** - Walk leads through the product/process (each card advances a story). Up to 10 images/videos with individual CTAs.
- **Short Video (15-30s)** + Static variants + CTA tests
- **Collection Ads** for multi-feature products

**Messaging Frameworks:**
- **Outcome-first:** "How to [result] in [timeframe]" format
- **Social proof overlay:** Screenshots of results, student testimonials, revenue dashboards
- **Lead magnet funnel:** Consideration objective > lead magnet > retarget with video testimonials
- **Direct-to-sale:** Works for sub-$50 offers with right creative + funnel (no long nurture needed)

**Creative Testing Protocol:**
- Build 6-10 creatives (mix of short video + static variants + CTA tests)
- Run 7-14 day tests monitoring CPA/ROAS
- Pause losers, scale winners with CBO lowest cost
- 92% of mobile users view video with sound off -- ALWAYS add subtitles

**Cost Benchmarks (2025):**
- Education CPL averages $19.27 (54% lower than global $41.53 average)
- Budget sweet spot: $50-500/day across 2-4 campaigns

**Best Static Formats for Infoproducts:**
1. Screenshot of course dashboard/content
2. "Before/After" results comparison
3. Mockup of the product (ebook cover, course interface)
4. Testimonial screenshot with face
5. Text-heavy "ugly ad" with bold promise + mechanism

## 4. UGC-STYLE STATIC ADS / "UGLY ADS"

### The Science of Organic-Looking Creatives

**Why They Work:**
- UGC boosts engagement by 28% over brand-created ads
- People are 2.4x more likely to engage with UGC
- 44% of consumers trust UGC more than any other content
- 56% more likely to click a user-generated image than traditional ad
- 70% of Americans more likely to buy after seeing relatable UGC image
- Meta confirmed: "native-feeling" UGC reduces ad fatigue

**Characteristics of High-Converting Ugly/UGC Statics:**
1. Natural lighting (not studio)
2. iPhone-quality photos (not DSLR-perfect)
3. Real environments (kitchen table, desk, outdoors) vs white backgrounds
4. Minimal branding (no logo in first frame, no custom fonts)
5. Handwritten or simple font overlays
6. Casual composition (slightly off-center, candid feel)
7. Social media native formatting (looks like an organic post)

**Static UGC Format Examples:**
- Phone screenshot of a conversation/result
- Selfie with text overlay
- Product in natural "messy desk" setting
- Handwritten note/list style
- Meme format with brand message
- Review/testimonial screenshot
- Before/after comparison photos

## 5. BARRY HOTT "UGLY ADS" METHODOLOGY

### Complete Framework

**Background:** $600M+ in social ad spend managed. Clients include AT&T, Toyota, True Classic, AG1, Harry's Razors.

**Core Principle:** "If your marketing is too pretty, it's probably not going to work on paid social."

**Performance Data:** Ugly ads achieve ~3x click rate and 3-5x higher conversion rate vs polished content. Lone Ranch Water campaign: 30% lift in action intent.

### The "Sniff Test" - Signs Your Ad is Over-Branded:
1. Heavy branding in first frame (logos, custom fonts, brand colors)
2. Perfectly lit scenes (obviously professional)
3. White/blank backgrounds vs natural settings
4. High video quality clearly shot on professional equipment
5. Heavily scripted dialogue vs authentic voice

### Barry Hott's 6 Rules:
1. **Start Small** - Modest budgets first, prove value, then scale
2. **Prioritize Authenticity** - Consumers detect "salesy" content instantly. Must feel like peer recommendation
3. **Commit to Format Expectations** - Use Impact font for memes, native captions for Reels. Small deviations trigger skepticism
4. **Enable Employee/Creator Content** - Internal contests, phone-only shoots, "weirdest product video" challenges
5. **Abandon Perfectionism** - Launch without over-polishing. iPhone while walking = authenticity
6. **Balance Relatability with Fundamentals** - Still need hook + story + CTA, but must feel like friends created it

### Ugly Ad Formats That Convert:
- Minimal-budget product demos
- Founder/CEO iPhone testimonials
- Meme-style educational content
- News-format product explanations
- Behind-the-scenes employee footage
- Screenshot-style statics
- Text-on-colored-background (no images at all)

### Critical Distinction:
**Ugly =/= Bad.** Ineffective ads fail from lacking attention, relevance, or relatability -- not production quality. Ugly ads STILL need compelling narrative structure and genuine persuasive power.

## 6. TEXT-HEAVY vs IMAGE-HEAVY

### Optimal Text-to-Image Ratio

**Current Status of 20% Rule (2025-2026):**
- Facebook no longer strictly enforces the 20% text rule
- BUT Meta still recommends low text-to-image ratio for better delivery
- Ads with minimal text consistently outperform text-heavy alternatives

**Meta's Internal Reach Impact Categories:**
| Text Density | Reach Impact |
|-------------|-------------|
| None (recommended) | Full reach potential |
| Low | Slightly reduced reach |
| Medium | Severely limited reach |
| High | Very limited reach |

**Best Practices:**
- Put main message in ad copy (primary text, headline), NOT on image
- Keep image text to 1-3 words max (hook/headline only)
- Exception: "Ugly ads" with intentionally text-heavy design CAN work because they look organic/native
- Text-only statics (no photos, just text on color) are a separate category that performs well

**The Paradox:**
- Meta penalizes text-heavy polished ads (looks like advertising)
- Meta does NOT penalize text-heavy ugly/native ads (looks like organic content)
- The algorithm detects "ad clutter" vs "native content" differently

**Recommendation for Thomas (Static Design):**
- Bold headline text: YES (large, impactful, 1-5 words)
- Body text on image: MINIMAL
- Put details in ad copy below
- Exception: screenshot/note-style ugly ads can be text-heavy

## 7. COLOR SCHEMES THAT CONVERT

### Color Psychology for Direct Response Ads

**Emotional Associations:**

| Color | Emotion/Association | Best For |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Red | Urgency, energy, passion, appetite | Flash sales, limited-time offers, urgency CTAs |
| Orange | Enthusiasm, action, creativity, friendliness | CTAs, action buttons, energy/excitement |
| Blue | Trust, calm, reliability, intelligence | Lead gen, financial products, credibility |
| Yellow | Optimism, youthfulness, friendliness | Attention-grabbing, positivity, fun products |
| Green | Health, harmony, growth, sustainability | Health products, "go" signals, eco/natural |
| Purple | Luxury, imagination, wisdom, uniqueness | Premium positioning, creativity |
| Black | Elegance, power, sophistication | Premium/luxury, authority |
| White | Simplicity, clarity, cleanliness | Clean layouts, breathing room |

### CTA Button Colors (Conversion Data):
- **Red vs Green:** HubSpot study showed red increased conversions by 21%
- **Orange & Blue** often outperform green for CTAs
- **Most popular high-converting CTA colors:** Orange, Blue, Red, Green (in that order per 90-button study)

### The Real Rule: CONTRAST > Color
**No single color universally converts best.** What matters most:
- How much the CTA/key element CONTRASTS with surrounding design
- Alignment with overall brand/design context
- One dominant color (emotional tone) + 1-2 accent colors (CTAs/highlights) + neutrals (breathing room)

### For Direct Response Ad Creatives:
- **Awareness:** Bold, high-contrast colors (increase visibility and memory)
- **Lead Generation:** Blue/green (trust, credibility, form completion)
- **Retargeting:** Familiar brand colors (recognition)
- **E-commerce/Sales:** Red/orange (urgency, action)

### For Zape Ads (Shopee ADS Course):
- Use EMOTIONAL colors per section (NOT Zape brand colors in ads)
- Urgency/scarcity: Red/orange
- Trust/credibility: Blue
- Results/money: Green/gold
- Premium/exclusivity: Black/dark tones
- Always A/B test color variants

## 8. AI AD CREATIVE PROMPT ENGINEERING AT SCALE

### How Agencies Use AI for Ad Creatives

**Scale Results:**
- Superside produced 3,300+ brand-aligned images for one client using Midjourney
- AI-generated banner ads achieve up to 50% higher CTR vs traditionally designed
- Design costs reduced by up to 40%, speed to market significantly faster

**Agency Workflow:**
1. Define brand style guide as "style seed/prompt"
2. Generate dozens of variations per concept (infinite A/B testing)
3. Hyper-personalize visuals for micro-segments
4. Maintain brand consistency by reusing style prompts across all content

### FLUX.2 Prompt Engineering for Ad Creatives

**Prompt Formula:** Subject + Action + Style + Context

**Key Rules:**
- NO negative prompts (FLUX.2 doesn't support them)
- Describe what you WANT, not what you don't want
- Word order matters - prioritize important elements first
- Use quotation marks for text: `"The text 'SALE' in bold red"`
- Use hex codes for precise colors: `"apple in color #FF5733"`
- Optimal length: 30-80 words for most projects

**FLUX.2 Photorealism Tips:**
```
Reference specific cameras: "shot on Sony A7IV, clean sharp, high dynamic range"
Vintage look: "shot on Kodak Portra 400, natural grain"
Specific lens: "Canon 5D Mark IV, 24-70mm at 35mm, golden hour, shallow depth of field"
```

**FLUX.2 Typography in Images:**
- Keep text to 1-4 words for legibility
- Place in quotes in prompt
- Describe a clear surface for text (sign, book cover, banner area)
- Describe font style: "elegant serif," "bold industrial," "handwritten script"
- Include color with hex: `"logo 'ZAPE' in color #A3E635"`

**FLUX.2 JSON Structured Prompting (for automation):**
```json
{
  "scene": "product showcase with clean background",
  "subjects": [{"description": "course dashboard on laptop screen", "position": "center"}],
  "style": "clean commercial photography",
  "color_palette": ["#A3E635", "#1a1a1a", "#ffffff"],
  "lighting": "soft studio lighting, slight shadow",
  "camera": {"angle": "slightly above", "lens": "50mm"}
}
```

**Aspect Ratios for Ads:**
- 1:1 - Social media feed, product shots
- 9:16 - Stories, Reels, mobile
- 4:5 - Instagram feed optimal
- 16:9 - Landscape/cinematic

## 9. PROMPTING FOR CLEAN TEXT OVERLAY AREAS

### How to Reserve Space for Text in AI-Generated Images

**Direct Approach (FLUX.2):**
```
Professional product photo with large clean empty area on the left side for
text overlay, [product] positioned on right third of frame, soft gradient
background transitioning from dark to light, studio lighting
```

**Compositional Approach:**
```
Rule-of-thirds composition with subject on right, expansive negative space
on left with smooth gradient background, suitable for typography placement,
commercial advertising photography style
```

**Background Gradient Approach:**
```
[Product/person] in lower right corner, vast clean gradient background from
[color1] to [color2] occupying upper two-thirds of frame, minimal
distracting elements, advertising layout
```

**Key Phrases That Work:**
- "clean negative space for text"
- "large empty area on [side] for typography"
- "advertising layout with text placement area"
- "commercial composition with copy space"
- "smooth gradient background suitable for text overlay"
- "editorial layout with breathing room"
- "banner-style composition with headline area"

## 10. PROMPTING FOR REALISTIC BRAZILIAN/LATIN PEOPLE

### Monk Skin Tone (MST) Scale for AI Prompts

Use this format: `skin tone: [anchor name] (MST [#]), [undertone] undertone`

**Relevant MST Levels for Brazilian Diversity:**

| MST | Anchor | Undertone Options | Brazilian Context |
|-----|--------|------------------|------------------|
| 3-4 | Light Sand, Beige | Cool neutral, Warm golden | Light-skinned Brazilian |
| 5 | Honey | Warm golden, Olive | Mixed heritage, common |
| 6 | Caramel | Warm golden, Neutral | Very common Brazilian tone |
| 7 | Bronze | Warm golden, Olive | Common darker Brazilian |
| 8 | Mahogany | Neutral, Warm golden | Dark-skinned Brazilian |

**Undertone Options:**
- Cool rosy (pink/red lean)
- Cool neutral (subtle cool)
- Neutral (balanced)
- Warm golden (yellow/golden lean) -- MOST COMMON for Brazilian/Latin
- Olive (green/grey lean) -- COMMON for Brazilian/Latin

### Prompt Templates for Realistic Brazilian People

**Basic Template:**
```
Candid photo of a Brazilian [man/woman], skin tone: caramel (MST 6), warm
golden undertone, [hair description], natural smile, wearing casual
[clothing], in a [Brazilian setting], shot on iPhone 14, natural lighting,
authentic and warm, editorial photography
```

**For Advertising:**
```
Professional yet approachable portrait of a [age] year old Brazilian
[man/woman], skin tone: honey (MST 5), warm golden undertone, dark wavy
hair, confident expression, [relevant action/context], clean background
with negative space for text overlay, commercial photography, shot on
Sony A7IV, soft natural lighting
```

**Important Rules:**
- NEVER use ethnicity as a shortcut (causes stereotyping)
- Describe CONCRETE facial features (nose shape, lip shape, face structure)
- Specify hair texture directly (coily, curly, wavy, straight)
- Add "100% photorealistic, not AI-generated looking" for quality
- Describe clothing and setting for cultural context
- For consistency across images: "keep same skin tone as reference (MST [#]), only change lighting"

**Alternative Descriptive Terms for Latin/Brazilian Skin:**
- dulce de leche, mocha, cafe au lait, burnt sugar, golden cinnamon
- olive, caramel, golden, toffee, amber, bronze
- For deeper tones: mocha, cocoa, coffee, mahogany, chestnut

### Quality Enhancement Phrases:
```
"natural skin texture with subtle pores and imperfections"
"authentic and warm, not overly retouched"
"real person, not a model, relatable and approachable"
"natural skin glow, slight perspiration in warm setting"
```

### Negative Prompt Alternatives (for models that support it):
```
Exclude: "plastic skin, porcelain doll, overly smooth, airbrushed,
uncanny valley, wax figure, mannequin, stock photo smile, generic face,
oversaturated skin, glowing skin, perfect symmetry"
```

## 11. COMPLETE NEGATIVE PROMPTS FOR AD CREATIVE QUALITY

### For FLUX.2 (no negative prompts - use positive phrasing):
Instead of "no watermark" say: `"clean image, professional photography, pristine"`
Instead of "no blur" say: `"sharp focus, crisp details, high resolution"`
Instead of "no text" say: `"clean image without any typography or lettering"`

### For Gemini/Midjourney/Stable Diffusion (negative prompts supported):

**Universal Quality Negatives:**
```
blurred, out of focus, low resolution, pixelated, noisy, grainy (unless
intentional), overexposed, underexposed, watermark, signature, logo,
amateur photography, snapshot quality
```

**For People/Portraits:**
```
deformed, disfigured, extra fingers, extra limbs, missing fingers,
mutated hands, bad anatomy, bad proportions, clone face, duplicate,
plastic skin, uncanny valley, wax figure, mannequin, cross-eyed,
asymmetric eyes
```

**For Product Photography:**
```
distorted product, warped edges, floating objects, impossible shadows,
inconsistent lighting, reflections showing studio, visible editing,
composite artifacts
```

**For Ad-Specific Quality:**
```
clipart, cartoon, illustration, vector art, 3D render (unless intended),
stock photo generic, cheesy, corporate generic, template-looking,
overdesigned, busy background, cluttered composition
```
