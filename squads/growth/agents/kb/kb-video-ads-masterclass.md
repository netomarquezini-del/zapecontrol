## PART 2: DECONSTRUCTING VIRAL AD FORMULAS

### Formula 1: "Dollar Shave Club" — Humor + Direct + Personality

**Core DNA:**
- Founder/personality IS the brand voice
- Humor serves the argument, never replaces it
- Every joke advances the value proposition
- Production quality deliberately low = authenticity signal
- Rapid pace leaves no room for disengagement

**Structure:**
1. HOOK (0-3s): Bold claim that stops scroll — "Our blades are f***ing great"
2. PROBLEM (3-10s): Why the status quo sucks — overpriced razors
3. PERSONALITY (10-30s): Rapid-fire jokes while walking through warehouse
4. PROOF (30-50s): Quick demos/absurd visuals reinforcing claims
5. CTA (50-60s): Direct, no-nonsense "stop paying for shave tech you don't need"

**When to use:** Low-to-mid ticket physical products, subscription services, anything where the founder has personality
**Adaptation for AI:** Generate script with founder archetype persona, rapid-fire joke structure, warehouse/office walkthrough format

### Formula 2: "Purple Mattress" — Absurd Demo + Mechanism

**Core DNA:**
- The VISUAL DEMONSTRATION is undeniable proof
- Humor wraps the demo so it's entertaining AND educational
- "Mechanism" (the raw egg test) becomes the brand's calling card
- Characters (Goldilocks, sasquatch) create memorable branding

**Structure:**
1. HOOK (0-3s): Intriguing visual that demands explanation
2. SETUP (3-15s): Introduce the "test" or "experiment"
3. DEMO (15-40s): Visual proof that can't be faked — eggs don't break
4. EXPLANATION (40-50s): Simple science of WHY it works (the mechanism)
5. CLOSE (50-60s): How to get it + reinforcement of the visual proof

**When to use:** Products with demonstrable physical advantages, anything with a unique mechanism
**Adaptation for AI:** Script the "impossible demo" concept, identify the product's visual proof moment

### Formula 3: "Squatty Potty" — Comedy + Education + Visual Metaphor

**Core DNA:**
- Uses absurd visual metaphor to explain a real product benefit
- The metaphor (unicorn pooping ice cream) makes the taboo topic SHAREABLE
- Real science embedded within entertainment
- Characters create brand identity beyond the ad

**Structure:**
1. HOOK (0-5s): Absurd visual that creates "WTF" curiosity
2. PROBLEM EDUCATION (5-20s): Real science about body mechanics
3. METAPHOR EXPANSION (20-40s): The unicorn/ice cream metaphor demonstrates the mechanism
4. PROOF (40-50s): Data/science backing up claims
5. CTA (50-60s): Simple, confident close

**When to use:** Taboo/awkward products, health products, anything needing education wrapped in entertainment
**Adaptation for AI:** Identify the product's "awkward truth," create metaphor that makes it discussable

### Formula 4: "Chatbooks" — Relatable Character Humor

**Core DNA:**
- Character represents the EXACT target customer (real mom, not idealized)
- Humor comes from TRUTH, not exaggeration
- Product solves a real, daily pain point shown in real-time chaos
- Warmth + humor + empathy = massive emotional connection

**Structure:**
1. HOOK (0-3s): Character in mid-chaos that target audience instantly recognizes
2. RELATABILITY (3-20s): Series of "that's SO me" moments
3. PRODUCT INTRO (20-35s): Natural discovery of product within the chaos
4. BENEFIT (35-50s): How product makes life easier, demonstrated not told
5. WARM CTA (50-60s): Invitation, not pressure

**When to use:** Products for parents, busy professionals, anyone with chaotic daily life
**Adaptation for AI:** Script character that mirrors target persona's daily reality, find humor in mundane truth

### Formula 5: "Poo-Pourri" — Taboo + Elegant Personality

**Core DNA:**
- JUXTAPOSITION is the engine — elegant persona discussing crude topic
- The persona's sophistication GIVES PERMISSION to discuss the taboo
- Deadpan delivery multiplies the humor
- The character IS the brand identity

**Structure:**
1. HOOK (0-3s): Beautiful, sophisticated scene — audience expects elegance
2. SUBVERSION (3-10s): Character breaks expectations with crude topic
3. HUMOR ESCALATION (10-30s): Increasingly vivid descriptions contrasting with elegance
4. PRODUCT (30-45s): Elegant solution to the crude problem
5. CHARACTER CTA (45-60s): Persona delivers CTA in-character

**When to use:** Taboo products, hygiene, health issues, anything embarrassing to discuss
**Adaptation for AI:** Create sophisticated persona archetype, write juxtaposition script

### Formula 6: "Old Spice" — Rapid-Fire Absurdist + Fourth Wall Break

**Core DNA:**
- Direct address to camera (fourth wall break) creates intimacy
- Impossible seamless transitions between locations/scenarios
- Self-aware parody of advertising tropes
- Aspirational masculinity turned into self-deprecating comedy
- Single-take format creates spectacle

**Structure:**
1. HOOK (0-2s): Direct eye contact + confident statement
2. ESCALATION (2-15s): Environment transforms impossibly while delivery continues unbroken
3. ABSURD PEAK (15-25s): Most ridiculous moment (diamonds, horse, etc.)
4. TAGLINE (25-33s): Self-aware callback + brand catchphrase

**When to use:** Lifestyle/personal care brands, anything targeting younger demo, brands wanting to shed "boring" image
**Adaptation for AI:** Script rapid-fire monologue with location transitions, specify practical effects vs. VFX

### Formula 7: "Apple" — Minimalist + Product-Centric + Aspirational

**Core DNA:**
- Product speaks for itself — minimal copy, maximum visual impact
- White space and silence are deliberate tools
- Shows OUTCOMES/RESULTS, never specs
- Users are the heroes, product is the enabler
- Aspiration without bragging

**Structure:**
1. VISUAL HOOK (0-3s): Stunning image/video that creates "wait, that's from a phone?"
2. PRODUCT IN ACTION (3-25s): User creating/capturing something beautiful
3. MINIMAL TEXT (25-28s): Single line — "Shot on iPhone" or similar
4. BRAND (28-30s): Apple logo. Nothing else needed

**When to use:** Premium products, creative tools, anything where the output IS the selling point
**Adaptation for AI:** Script the "outcome showcase" format, identify user-created content as proof

### Formula 8: "Nike" — Emotional Storytelling + Identity

**Core DNA:**
- NEVER sells product — sells the IDENTITY of the person who uses it
- Emotional narrative about human struggle and perseverance
- Inclusive messaging — "if they can, I can"
- Music and narration carry the emotional weight
- Product appears only in final seconds (or not at all)

**Structure:**
1. EMOTIONAL HOOK (0-5s): Person facing adversity/challenge
2. STRUGGLE (5-20s): Montage of effort, setback, determination
3. BREAKTHROUGH (20-40s): Moment of triumph
4. UNIVERSAL MESSAGE (40-55s): "Just Do It" — applicable to ANY viewer's life
5. BRAND (55-60s): Logo only. No CTA. Identity IS the CTA

**When to use:** Lifestyle brands, fitness, education, anything where the buyer's IDENTITY matters more than the product
**Adaptation for AI:** Script emotional narrative arc, identify target audience's core struggle

## PART 3: E-COMMERCE SPECIFIC WINNER FORMATS

### Format 1: The "Unboxing Reveal"

**Why it works:** Triggers ownership simulation — viewer's brain processes the unboxing as if THEY are opening it. Creates anticipation through delayed gratification.

**Structure:**
- 0-2s: Package arrives / hands holding sealed box
- 2-5s: First peek — partial reveal creating curiosity
- 5-15s: Full unboxing — show packaging quality, included items
- 15-25s: First reaction — genuine excitement/surprise
- 25-30s: Product in use — immediate demonstration

**Key elements:**
- ASMR sounds (tape peeling, paper crinkling) increase watch time
- Close-up hands-only shots increase intimacy
- Multiple camera angles maintain visual interest
- Text overlays highlight key features during reveal
- Works especially well for: premium products, subscription boxes, gifts

**Performance benchmarks:** Unboxing videos get 45% more engagement than standard product demos

### Format 2: The "TikTok Made Me Buy It"

**Why it works:** Social proof + native format + curiosity. Viewer thinks "if it went viral, it must be good."

**Structure:**
- 0-2s: "OK so TikTok made me buy this..." (text + voice)
- 2-5s: Show the product/package
- 5-15s: First impression/reaction
- 15-25s: Actual use demonstration
- 25-30s: Verdict + "and honestly..."

**Key elements:**
- Must feel 100% organic — no brand logos until end
- Skepticism in the opening increases credibility
- Real setting (bedroom, kitchen, bathroom)
- Honest tone — can mention minor negatives to increase trust
- End with strong positive that overcomes any negatives mentioned

### Format 3: The "Honest Review"

**Why it works:** 85% of people find UGC more trustworthy than brand-created media. The "honesty" frame disarms sales resistance.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "Honest review of [product]" (direct to camera, casual setting)
- 3-8s: What you expected vs. what you got
- 8-15s: The GOOD — specific, not generic praise
- 15-20s: The "BAD" — minor criticism that increases credibility
- 20-25s: The verdict — strong positive outweighing the minor negative
- 25-30s: "Would I buy again? 100%"

**Key elements:**
- The "negative" must be genuinely minor (packaging, shipping time)
- Specific details ("the texture is..." not "it's great")
- Close-up product shots during review
- Rating system (8.5/10 is more credible than 10/10)

### Format 4: The "Product Comparison"

**Why it works:** Addresses buyer uncertainty directly. Shows advantages instead of letting customers research competitors.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "I tested [product] vs [competitor]" (split-screen or side-by-side)
- 3-10s: Side-by-side physical comparison
- 10-20s: Performance test — specific, measurable
- 20-25s: Clear winner declaration with evidence
- 25-30s: "Link in bio" or direct CTA

**Key elements:**
- Must be genuinely fair — obvious bias kills credibility
- Use specific metrics (lasted 2x longer, 30% lighter)
- Show the actual test, don't just claim results
- Price comparison if favorable

### Format 5: The "Day in My Life + Product"

**Why it works:** Product becomes part of aspirational lifestyle, not interrupting ad. Viewer watches for the lifestyle content, absorbs the product placement.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "A day in my life as a [identity]"
- 3-10s: Morning routine — product appears naturally
- 10-20s: Key moments of the day — product solves a real moment
- 20-25s: End of day reflection — product mentioned as essential
- 25-30s: Soft CTA or product tag

**Key elements:**
- Product must feel NATURAL, not forced
- Lifestyle must match target audience's aspiration
- Multiple touchpoints — product appears 2-3 times naturally
- Aesthetic quality matters — aspirational visuals

### Format 6: The "Transformation / Results"

**Why it works:** Before-and-after is the most primal persuasion format. Visual proof of change is irrefutable. Gets 45% more engagement than static posts.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "BEFORE" state — relatable problem shown visually
- 3-5s: Transition moment — product introduction
- 5-15s: Process montage — showing the transformation happening
- 15-25s: "AFTER" state — dramatic visual difference
- 25-30s: Product callout + CTA

**Key elements:**
- Same lighting/angle for before and after (credibility)
- Time-lapse elements for longer transformations
- Specific metrics ("30 days later," "after 2 weeks")
- Real person, real results — not stock

### Format 7: The "Behind the Scenes"

**Why it works:** Creates transparency and trust. "If they show how it's made, they must be confident in quality."

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "Ever wonder how [product] is made?"
- 3-15s: Factory/workshop footage — raw, authentic
- 15-25s: Key quality moments highlighted
- 25-30s: Finished product + CTA

### Format 8: The "Customer Reaction"

**Why it works:** Genuine surprise/delight is contagious. Viewer mirrors the emotional reaction.

**Structure:**
- 0-2s: Customer receiving/opening product (anticipation)
- 2-10s: Genuine reaction — surprise, delight, laughter
- 10-20s: Customer explaining why they love it (specific details)
- 20-30s: "Where did you get this?" moment + CTA

### Format 9: The "Problem/Hack"

**Why it works:** Opens with a universal problem (identification), then presents product as the "hack" or "solution you didn't know existed."

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "Why does no one talk about THIS problem?"
- 3-8s: Show the problem in relatable way
- 8-15s: "I found this hack..." — introduce product as discovery
- 15-25s: Demo the solution working
- 25-30s: "Trust me, you need this" + CTA

### Format 10: The "Gift Guide"

**Why it works:** Removes purchase barrier — "it's not for me, it's a gift." Also creates urgency around seasonal moments.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "Gift ideas for [persona] under [price]"
- 3-20s: Quick showcase of 3-5 products (one is THE product)
- 20-25s: THE product gets slightly more screen time
- 25-30s: "Link for everything in bio"

## PART 4: INFO-PRODUCT / COURSE AD FORMATS

### CRITICAL CONTEXT FOR NETO'S MARKET
- Neto sells: Shopee ADS 2.0 (low-ticket course), Programa de Aceleracao (R$12K), Consultoria (R$42K)
- Avatar: 25-40, class C-B, sellers wanting to scale on marketplaces
- Tone: Technical + educational, like a professor who simplifies complexity
- Proof: 4,000+ students, 400+ mentored, R$50M+ in marketplace sales generated

### Format 1: "Free Value → Want More"

**Why it works:** Demonstrates expertise IN the ad. Viewer thinks: "If the FREE content is this good, imagine the paid course."

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: Hook — "A estrategia que fez meu aluno sair de 0 pra R$200K em 4 meses" (specific result)
- 3-20s: Teach ONE genuine tactical insight — must be actionable
- 20-25s: "Isso e so 1% do que eu ensino no [course name]"
- 25-30s: CTA to course page

**BR Adaptation:**
- Use specific Shopee/marketplace terminology
- Reference ROAS numbers (25x ROAS grabs attention)
- Show actual Shopee dashboard screenshots (blurred revenue ok)
- Portuguese: conversational, "voce" not "voces," avoid formality

**Key elements:**
- The value given must be REAL — not teaser, not vague
- One specific tactic they can implement today
- Then frame the course as "the complete system"
- Social proof: "Mais de 4.000 alunos ja aplicam isso"

### Format 2: "Student Transformation"

**Why it works:** The most powerful format for course ads. Real students with real results = irrefutable proof. 52.5% of consumers consider testimonials decisive for purchase.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: Student on camera: "Eu vendia R$5K por mes na Shopee..."
- 3-10s: The "before" — struggles, frustrations, what they tried
- 10-20s: The turning point — "Ai eu conheci o metodo do Neto..."
- 20-25s: The "after" — specific results with numbers
- 25-30s: "Se eu consegui, voce tambem consegue" + CTA

**Key elements:**
- Must be unscripted or feel unscripted
- Specific numbers: "Sai de R$5K pra R$200K em 4 meses"
- Emotional moment: the WHY behind wanting to succeed
- Film in student's natural environment (home office, estoque)
- Multiple students in rapid succession = compounded proof

### Format 3: "Methodology Reveal"

**Why it works:** Names the system (Metodo TRIA) and reveals just enough to create intellectual curiosity. The framework name becomes an asset.

**Structure:**
- 0-3s: "Eu criei um metodo que meus alunos chamam de TRIA"

## PART 5: THE "IMPOSSIBLE TO SCROLL PAST" ELEMENTS

### Visual Elements That Physically Stop the Scroll

**Decision Window:** You have 1.5-1.7 seconds to stop the thumb. 71% of viewers decide in first 3 seconds. The brain starts processing at 400 milliseconds.

**1. High Contrast Against Platform Background**
- Bright yellow/orange on white feed = instant visibility
- Neon colors on dark backgrounds
- Red is the most attention-grabbing color in feeds
- Avoid blues/grays — they blend with Facebook/Instagram UI

**2. Human Faces With Strong Emotion**
- Eyes looking directly at camera = strongest thumb-stop
- Expressions of surprise, shock, or excitement
- Close-up faces outperform wide shots for thumb-stop
- Person "mid-action" (mouth open, pointing, reacting)

**3. Motion in First Frame**
- Sudden movement in first 0.5 seconds
- Rapid zoom in/out
- Object entering frame unexpectedly
- Person turning to face camera (as if "catching" them scrolling)

**4. Visual Pattern Interrupt**
- Something that doesn't "belong" in the feed (upside-down image, black screen, etc.)
- Split-screen comparisons (immediate curiosity)
- Products in unexpected contexts
- Destruction/breaking something (Blendtec principle)

**5. The "Messy First Frame" Technique**
- Deliberately imperfect/ugly opening frame
- Looks like organic content, not an ad
- Barry Hott "ugly ads" principle — polished = ad = skip

**Performance Data:**
- Videos with pattern interrupt in first 3s see 3x higher engagement
- Effective first-frame ads get 65% longer watch time
- 41% higher completion rates when hook lands in first 3s

### Sound Elements That Arrest Attention

**1. Audio Hooks in First 1 Second**
- Dramatic sound effect (record scratch, glass breaking, notification "ding")
- Unexpected voice tone (whisper then shout, or vice versa)
- ASMR sounds (tapping, crinkling, pouring) — highly effective for product ads
- A rhythm/beat — brain responds faster to rhythm than visuals

**2. Voice Hooks**
- Start mid-sentence: "—and THAT'S why I stopped using [competitor]"
- Whisper-to-normal voice transition
- Question directed at viewer: "Be honest — have you ever [problem]?"
- Dramatic pause after first word

**3. Music Strategy**
- Trending TikTok sounds = algorithmic boost + familiarity
- Instrumental only for voiceover/talking-head ads (vocals compete)
- Custom 5-second "Audio Logo" — consistent brand sound at ad start
- Silence as a pattern interrupt (in a feed full of sound)

**4. Sound On/Off Strategy**
- Design for SOUND OFF first — text overlays must carry full message
- Sound ON is a bonus that enhances, not essential
- Captions/subtitles are mandatory, not optional
- Bold text on screen mimics the audio for sound-off viewers

### Text Elements That Create Irresistible Curiosity

**1. The Curiosity Gap**
- "The thing about [X] that nobody tells you..."
- "I was wrong about [X] for 5 years"
- "This ONE change made me [specific result]"
- Opens a gap between what they know and what they want to know
- Psychology: partial information creates an "itch" the brain MUST scratch

**2. Specific Numbers**
- "R$200K em 4 meses" > "milhares de reais"
- "25x de ROAS" > "retorno alto"
- "3 segundos" > "rapidamente"
- Specific numbers are visually striking AND more credible

**3. Contrarian Statements**
- "Stop running Shopee ADS immediately" (then: "...until you learn THIS")
- "Your ROAS doesn't matter" (then: "...if you're ignoring THIS metric")
- Creates cognitive dissonance that demands resolution

**4. Identity Hooks**
- "If you sell on Shopee and still don't use ADS..."
- "Every seller who fatures R$100K+ already knows this"
- Viewer self-identifies and feels personally addressed

**5. Text-on-Screen Best Practices**
- Maximum 7 words per text overlay
- High contrast text with shadow/outline for readability
- Animate text (fade in, typewriter effect, bounce)
- Position in top-third for Instagram, center for TikTok
- Action overlays ("Shop now," "Learn more") increase conversion 18%+

### Emotional Triggers That Override the Scroll Impulse

**1. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)**
- 60% of impulse purchases driven by FOMO
- "Ultimas vagas" / "So ate [date]"
- Show others already succeeding (social proof + FOMO combo)
- Timer/countdown visible in ad

**2. Identity Aspiration**
- "Become the seller who..." / "Seja o vendedor que..."
- Show the lifestyle/identity they want to achieve
- Product purchase = shortcut to identity transformation
- Nike/Apple principle: sell who they'll BECOME, not what they'll GET

**3. Belonging**
- "Join 4,000+ sellers who already..." / "Junte-se a mais de 4.000..."
- Community element — they're joining a group, not just buying a product
- Exclusive access framing

**4. Righteous Anger**
- "Voce esta perdendo dinheiro todo dia por causa DISSO"
- Channel frustration at the problem/status quo
- Position yourself as ally AGAINST the common enemy

**5. Hope / Transformation**
- "Imagine abrir o painel da Shopee e ver esse numero..."
- Future-pacing — help them visualize the desired outcome
- Student success stories that make them think "could be me"

### The "Pattern Break in the Feed" Technique

**What it is:** Making your ad so visually/aurally different from surrounding organic content that the brain's pattern-matching system flags it as novel.

**Techniques:**
1. **Black screen with white text** (0.5s) before video starts
2. **Loud sound then silence** — auditory whiplash
3. **Upside-down video** that rights itself
4. **Breaking the fourth wall** — "Hey, I know you're scrolling, but..."
5. **Fake interface elements** — look like a notification, message, or system alert
6. **Extreme close-up** that zooms out to reveal context
7. **Split-screen contradiction** — two things that shouldn't be together

**Performance Data:**
- Pattern interrupts in first 3 seconds: 3x engagement
- 65% longer watch times
- 41% higher completion rates
- The 0.8-1.2 second mark is the critical window for visual jolt

### First-Frame Strategies That Maximize Thumb-Stop Rate

**The Golden Rules:**
1. Lead with PRODUCT, VALUE, or HIGH-CONTRAST DESIGN in frame one
2. NEVER start with logo or brand intro — signals "ad" and invites skip
3. Put the PAYOFF in frame one — not a build-up
4. Human face with direct eye contact = highest thumb-stop
5. Text overlay visible in frame one (not animated in later)

**Frame-One Archetypes:**
1. **The Shocked Face** — person mid-reaction, mouth open
2. **The Impossible Scene** — something that shouldn't be possible
3. **The Big Number** — R$200K filling the screen
4. **The Question** — bold text asking something they must answer
5. **The Problem** — visual representation of pain point
6. **The Mystery Object** — what IS that? (curiosity)
7. **The Before** — ugly/messy state that implies transformation is coming

## PART 6: AD CREATIVE FRAMEWORKS FROM TOP AGENCIES / MEDIA BUYERS

### Framework 1: Pilothouse — The 3-3-3 System

**Who:** Performance marketing agency, $1B+ in direct revenue for clients

**The Framework:**
- **3 Concepts:** Each testing period, test 3 distinct creative CONCEPTS (not variations)
- **3 Formats per Concept:** For each concept, create 3 different FORMAT executions (e.g., UGC, static, video)
- **3 Hooks per Format:** For each format, test 3 different HOOKS

**Total:** 3 x 3 x 3 = 27 creative variations per testing cycle

**Why it works:** Ensures "range" and "diversity" in the ad account without over-complicating. Systematic enough to find winners, simple enough to execute.

**Kill criteria:** Clear rules for when to kill underperformers and scale winners.

### Framework 2: Barry Hott — "Ugly Ads" System

**Who:** $600M+ in managed social ad spend (AT&T, Toyota, Kraft, Microsoft)

**Core Principle:** If your marketing looks like marketing, most people skip it. "Marketing blindness" means polished ads get ignored.

**The System:**
1. **Start ugly:** iPhone-shot, no fancy editing, organic feel
2. **Hook test first:** If hook rate is high, iterate on body/CTA keeping the hook
3. **Modular testing:** Change ONE element at a time — hook, body, offer, CTA
4. **Data-driven iteration:** Every test based on data, not creative intuition
5. **Ugly Figma templates:** Pre-built templates that look native, not polished

**Key insight:** "If something looks like an ad and feels like an ad, a majority of people are probably not going to look at it."

### Framework 3: Common Thread Collective (CTC)

**Who:** E-commerce growth agency, known for rigorous creative testing

**Approach:**
1. **Structured creative tests** to arrive at actionable insights monthly
2. **Concept → Variation → Iteration** pipeline
3. **Winners get variations** — never vary losers
4. **Data review cadence:** Weekly analysis, monthly strategic shifts, quarterly resets
5. **Integration of creative + media buying** — creative strategy informed by performance data

### Framework 4: VaynerMedia — "Day Trading Attention" Model

**Content Model:**

### The Modular Creative System (Industry Standard)

**Core Architecture:**
Every ad = HOOK + BODY + CTA — each is independently testable.

**Hook Module (First 3-5 seconds):**
- Test 10-15 hook variations per concept
- Measure by 3-second view rate and thumb-stop ratio
- Top 2-3 hooks advance to body testing

**Body Module (5-25 seconds):**
- Test 5-7 body variations with winning hooks
- Types: product demo, testimonial, education, storytelling, comparison
- Measure by watch-through rate and engagement

**CTA Module (Last 5 seconds):**
- Test 4-5 CTA variations with winning hook+body combos
- Types: urgency, social proof, direct ask, soft sell, value stack
- Measure by conversion rate and CPA

**Combinatorial Math:**
- 7 hooks x 5 bodies x 4 proofs x 3 CTAs = 420 theoretical combinations
- In practice: 50-100 coherent variations from one framework
- Data shows 5-7 creatives per campaign = 1.5x performance boost
- TikTok research: weekly creative refresh = 10-12% more conversions

**The Winners Hub:**
- Documented library of proven creative elements
- Catalog: winning hooks, high-performing bodies, effective CTAs, visual styles, messaging angles
- New campaigns remix proven components, never start from zero
- Compounding learning loop: each test cycle raises baseline performance

## APPENDIX B: AI VIDEO CREATION AGENT — PROGRAMMING DIRECTIVES

### Decision Tree for Format Selection

```
IF product_type == "info_product" AND ticket == "low":
  → "Free Value" format OR "Myth Busting" format
  → Hook: Resultado/Curiosidade
  → Body: One tactical insight + "isso e so 1%"
  → CTA: Direct to sales page

IF product_type == "info_product" AND ticket == "high":
  → "Student Transformation" OR "Live Coaching Clip" OR "Methodology Reveal"
  → Hook: Prova Social/Transformacao
  → Body: Emotional journey + specific numbers
  → CTA: WhatsApp or application page

IF product_type == "ecommerce_physical":
  → "Unboxing" OR "Honest Review" OR "Problem/Hack"
  → Hook: Product-in-hand or big visual
  → Body: Demo + specific benefits
  → CTA: Shop now link

IF product_type == "ecommerce_digital" OR "SaaS":
  → "Before/After" OR "Product Comparison" OR "Free Value"
  → Hook: Dashboard screenshot or transformation
  → Body: Feature demo + outcome
  → CTA: Free trial or sign up

IF awareness_level == "unaware":
  → Problem-first hooks, education body
IF awareness_level == "problem_aware":
  → Agitation + solution tease
IF awareness_level == "solution_aware":
  → Mechanism/method differentiation
IF awareness_level == "product_aware":
  → Social proof + urgency
IF awareness_level == "most_aware":
  → Direct offer + scarcity
```

### Quality Checklist for Every Video Ad

```
[ ] Hook stops scroll in <3 seconds
[ ] Works with sound OFF (text overlays carry message)
[ ] Specific numbers used (not vague claims)
[ ] One clear CTA (not multiple)
[ ] Native to platform (doesn't look like "an ad")
[ ] Emotional trigger present (FOMO, identity, belonging, hope)
[ ] First frame has high contrast + visual interest
[ ] Body delivers value, not just hype
[ ] Social proof included (number, testimonial, or result)
[ ] Compliant with Meta policies (no income guarantees, no before/after health claims)
[ ] Vertical format (9:16) for Reels/Stories/TikTok
[ ] Duration appropriate: 15-30s for awareness, 30-60s for conversion
[ ] Captions/subtitles present and readable
[ ] Pattern interrupt in first 1.5 seconds
```
